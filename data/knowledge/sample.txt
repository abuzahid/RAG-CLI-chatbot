# Life Insurance Knowledge Base

## Term Life Insurance

Term life insurance provides coverage for a specific period, typically 10, 20, or 30 years.
It is the most affordable type of life insurance and pays a death benefit if the insured
passes away during the term.

Key features of term life insurance:
- Fixed premium payments for the term period
- Death benefit paid to beneficiaries if the insured dies during the term
- No cash value accumulation
- Can be converted to permanent insurance in some cases

Best for: Young families, people with temporary financial obligations, those on a budget

## Whole Life Insurance

Whole life insurance is a type of permanent life insurance that provides coverage for
your entire lifetime, as long as premiums are paid.

Key features of whole life insurance:
- Level premiums that never increase
- Guaranteed death benefit
- Cash value accumulation that grows over time
- Potential to earn dividends (for participating policies)

Best for: Long-term financial planning, estate planning, those wanting guaranteed coverage

## Universal Life Insurance

Universal life insurance is a flexible permanent policy that combines a death benefit
with a cash value component that earns interest.

Key features of universal life insurance:
- Flexible premium payments
- Adjustable death benefit
- Cash value grows based on current interest rates
- Policy loans can be taken against cash value

Best for: Those wanting flexibility in premiums and death benefit

## Coverage Eligibility

Generally, to qualify for life insurance:
- Age: Typically 18-75 years old
- Health: May require medical exam or health questionnaire
- Lifestyle: Non-smokers get better rates
- Occupation: High-risk jobs may affect premiums

## Filing a Claim

To file a life insurance claim:
1. Notify the insurance company promptly after the insured's death
2. Obtain the death certificate
3. Complete the claim form provided by the insurer
4. Submit required documents (death certificate, policy information, beneficiary ID)
5. Wait for processing (typically 30-60 days)

Required documents typically include:
- Certified death certificate
- Original policy document
- Claimant's statement
- Beneficiary identification

## Beneficiaries

A beneficiary is the person or entity who receives the death benefit. You can name:
- Primary beneficiary: First in line to receive benefits
- Contingent beneficiary: Receives benefits if primary beneficiary cannot

Tips for naming beneficiaries:
- Keep beneficiary designations updated
- Consider using percentages for multiple beneficiaries
- Name specific individuals rather than "my children"
- Review beneficiaries after major life events
